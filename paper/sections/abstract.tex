\begin{abstract}
Deep learning models deployed in safety-critical settings can fail silently under distribution shift, producing confident but unreliable outputs with no mechanism for self-diagnosis. We study this through seismic phase picking, showing that PhaseNet's P-wave detection rate drops from 85.5\% to 35.1\% between California and Kazakhstan while confidence distributions remain nearly identical, rendering standard confidence-based deferral ineffective. We develop a regime-aware deferral framework that combines an external Hidden Markov Model tracking the seismic environment with Beta-Bernoulli reliability estimation, producing a deferral score that integrates environmental uncertainty with model confidence. The regime-aware score concentrates 90.9\% of deferrals during seismically active periods (vs.\ 36.4\% for confidence-only), directing scarce human analyst time to the moments of greatest environmental uncertainty---though confidence-only deferral achieves higher raw system accuracy at matched deferral rates. The framework demonstrates a general principle for safety-critical ML: when model self-assessment fails under distribution shift, structured external context can provide the reliability signal that internal uncertainty cannot.
\end{abstract}
